Our Four Selection Standards

Not every good hotel is a good hotel for a woman on her own. We evaluate every property against four non-negotiable criteria before it earns a place on this site.

Safe at Night

Well-lit corridors, reliable room locks, 24-hour front desk, and a neighbourhood where you can walk back from dinner without calculating risk. If we wouldn't take a late evening stroll nearby, it doesn't make the list.

Comfortable & Quiet

Thick walls, quality bedding, proper blackout curtains, and bathrooms designed for adults. No party hostels rebranded as boutiques. Sleep matters — especially when you're crossing time zones alone.

Respect as Standard

Staff who treat a woman dining alone or checking in solo with the same warmth and attention as any couple or group. No raised eyebrows, no pity, no second-class service. Respect is the baseline, not a bonus.

Easy Access

Close to public transit, walkable to restaurants and cultural sites, with clear communication in English or multilingual staff. No beautiful-but-isolated properties that leave you stranded without a car or a plan.

Hotel Guides by City

Each city guide features hand-picked hotels that meet all four standards. Honest descriptions, real neighbourhood context, and the details that matter when you're booking for yourself.

Lisbon

Boutique gems in Principe Real and Chiado where solo women are welcomed warmly. Expect tiled facades, rooftop terraces, and staff who remember your name by day two.

  • Boutique
  • Walkable
  • Great Value

Tokyo

From immaculate business hotels in Marunouchi to serene ryokan-style stays in Yanaka, Tokyo delivers precision hospitality where a woman alone is never an afterthought.

  • Ultra Safe
  • Impeccable Service
  • Transit Connected

Barcelona

Elegant mid-range hotels in Eixample and Gothic Quarter, steps from galleries and late-night tapas. Mediterranean light, art-filled lobbies, and a culture that never rushes you.

  • Culture Rich
  • Central
  • Terrace Dining

Mexico City

Design-forward hotels in Roma Norte and Condesa with lush courtyards, rooftop pools, and neighbourhoods where evening walks feel easy. Exceptional value for the quality.

  • Design Forward
  • Affordable
  • Vibrant Neighbourhood

Buenos Aires

Converted mansions in Palermo and Recoleta where European elegance meets Argentine warmth. Late check-outs, wine-friendly lobbies, and concierges who know which milonga welcomes beginners.

  • Character
  • Late-Night Friendly
  • Wine Culture

Paris

Left Bank charm and Right Bank polish — classic Parisian hotels where a woman reading alone at breakfast is the most natural sight in the world. Neighbourhood picks from Saint-Germain to Le Marais.

  • Classic
  • Solo Dining
  • Walkable
